# Draft Blog: The Sales Rep RoofClaw: Turning Local Service Leads Into Booked Opportunities

RoofClaw started as a dedicated AI Chief of Staff for roofing businesses: local hardware, secure setup, real memory, real tools, and a direct line to the owner.

But a new opportunity is opening up.

With Google Local Services Ads lead access, HubSpot CRM integration, OpenClaw voice calling, iMessage/BlueBubbles support, and email workflows, a properly configured RoofClaw can now act like a front-line sales rep for roofing leads.

Not a chatbot. Not a browser tab. A dedicated sales operator.

## What it does

A Sales Rep RoofClaw can help:

- Respond quickly to Google Local Services Ads leads
- Qualify roofing requests
- Create and update HubSpot contacts/deals
- Follow up by approved channels: LSA message, phone, text/iMessage, and email
- Escalate complex or sensitive situations to a human
- Keep the CRM clean without waiting on the sales team

## Why it matters

Roofing lead response speed is money. The contractor who responds first, clearly, and professionally usually has the advantage.

Most roofing companies do not lose because they lack effort. They lose because leads arrive across too many channels, at too many times, with too many handoffs.

A Sales Rep RoofClaw gives the business a dedicated revenue desk that never forgets, never loses the lead, and never leaves HubSpot dirty.

## When not to install it

This part matters.

Do not bolt this onto a RoofClaw that was configured as a Chief of Staff, Production Coordinator, Operations agent, Bookkeeper, Recruiter, or Support agent.

Could one agent technically do all of that? Sometimes.

Should it? Usually no.

Role clarity is what keeps an agent useful. A Sales Rep RoofClaw needs different scripts, permissions, memory, escalation rules, KPIs, and urgency than a production or executive agent.

If your existing RoofClaw is not sales-focused, the clean recommendation is a dedicated Sales RoofClaw V2.0.

Existing RoofClaw customers receive 25% off a Sales RoofClaw V2.0.

If you are not ready for a second RoofClaw yet, the right move is simple: make the first one so economically valuable that the second one becomes obvious.

## What you need

To use the open-source launchpad, you need:

- A Sales-configured RoofClaw
- HubSpot
- Google Local Services Ads access
- Approved sales scripts
- Clear escalation rules
- Optional phone/text/iMessage/email setup
- A willingness to start in dry-run mode before going live

## Open source launchpad

We published the launchpad under the MIT license here:

`https://github.com/Roofing-Business-Partner/roofclaw-sales-rep-hubspot`

Point your Sales RoofClaw at the repo and ask it to start with `LAUNCHPAD.md`.

If your RoofClaw is not sales-configured, it should reject the install and tell you why.

That is not friction. That is discipline.

The goal is not to make your agent busier.

The goal is to make your business faster, cleaner, and more profitable.
